Reading
"What is the dream actually about?"
Family is present but distorted (mother age-inverted), reachable but uncommunicable (brother behind a foreign alphabet), and finally absent (the empty beach). Every familiar surface in the dream substitutes itself for a slipperier material: plaster for water, wood for sand. The closing image — late for something forgotten — is the dream's emotional center: urgency without object. The thing the dreamer is late for has the same status as everything else in the dream — it once had a name, and the name has come off.
